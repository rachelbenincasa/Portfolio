Why Computer Science?

When students learn computer science skills, they are not only learning how to code, but how to think critically and solve problems. These skills are applicable to a wide range of subjects, not just technical ones. CS education is also important because as CS education grows, so do the opportunities for students to create a space for themselves in the computing field. Now more than ever, it is critical to ensure all students have the chance to learn computational thinking.

The CoderKids Promise

CoderKids has one simple mission: to teach computing fundamentals in a way that grabs kids' attention. We believe in tapping into students' existing skills and passions through our curriculum. Our courses focus on teaching computer science as a tool to explore their interests, rather than an entirely different subject altogether. Whether your student loves sports, fashion, games, writing, or all of the above: they can use computer science to further that passion.

Our Philosophy

We believe in empowering students to become problem solvers and ground breakers in the things they love. Computer science skills are only as powerful as the ideas and passion behind the screen. We emphasize the following principles:

  • Hands-on Learning: We value practical, experiential learning that allows students to learn by doing. Students strengthen their skills through hands-on projects and challenges.
  • Collaboration: We foster a collaborative learning environment where students work together and share ideas. We teach our students to value different backgrounds, experiences, and interests.
  • Diversity and Inclusion: We are committed to promoting diversity and inclusion in the field of computing. CoderKids welcomes students and families of all backgrounds and strive for a welcoming and inclusive learning environment.
  • Lifelong Learning: The field of tech is always growing, and we teach our students to be open-minded about the doors that programming skills can open for them.
